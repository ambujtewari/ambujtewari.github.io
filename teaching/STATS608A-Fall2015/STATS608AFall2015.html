Class Information

  • Days & Time: Mondays and Wednesdays, 4-5:30pm (This half-semester course begins on October 26, 2015.)
  • Location: 1372 East Hall
  • Description: This is a graduate course on convex optimization with a focus on applications in machine learning and statistics. We will discuss several major families of optimization algorithms such as subgradient methods, proximal algorithms and coordinate descent methods.
  • Textbook: There’s no required textbook. For reference, we will use Convex Optimization Algorithms by Dimitri P. Bertsekas and Convex Optimization by Boyd and Vandenberghe.
  • Ctools: You should access the Canvas class page for this course frequently. It will contain important announcements and posted homework assignments.

Grading

The final grade in the course will be determined by your scores in 3 homeworks and one final exam using the weights given below.

  • Homeworks (75%): There will be 3 equally weighted homework assignments. The problems on the homeworks will be a mix of theoretical and programming problems. There is no “official” programming language for the course. Knowledge of Python/Matlab/R should be sufficient to solve problems.
  • HW 1 (out: Nov 4, due: Nov 11)
  • HW 2 (out: Nov 11, due: Nov 23)
  • HW 3 (out: Nov 25, due: Dec 5)
  • Course project (25%): Projects will have to be done in pairs (I will permit a group of 3 if the scope of the proposed project is wider). I will post some project ideas but you should feel free to choose a project topic close to your research. Project evaluation will be based on:
  • a 2-pages long project proposal (5%), and
  • a 6-8 pages long final report (20%) in the NIPS 2015 format.
